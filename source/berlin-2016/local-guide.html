Kreuzberg District

With it’s legendary bars and the best Kebabs in town, Kreuzberg is a lively district of Berlin. To walk there leave Radial System and turn right to cross bridge next to Ibis hotel. Follow the street and turn left at Adalbertstraße and after about 10 – 15 minutes you have reached the heart of Kreuzberg. The area around ar Markthalle Neun (Eisenbahnstraße 42-43) in particular has a lot to offer.

Friedrichshain Area

Friedrichshain is now considered one of Berlin’s most fashionable areas boasting a great choice of restaurants in all price ranges as well as many charming cafés. To walk there leave Radial System and walk along East-Side Gallery (or take the S-Bahn to Warschauer Straße), turn left cross the bridge to head north and get lost in this vibrant East-Berlin neighbourhood.

Local Attractions / Activities

In addition to Checkpoint Charlie, Brandenburg Gate, Deutscher Bundestag (Reichstag) and the Holocaust Memorial, we recommend a few lesser-known local attractions.

East Side gallery
105 paintings by artists from all over the world, painted in 1990 on the east side of the Berlin Wall (and close to Radialsystem).

Mauerpark
Open every Sunday, Flohmarkt am Mauerpark (Mauerpark Flea Market) is popular with both locals and tourists. The park straddles a stretch of the former Berlin Wall.

Berlin Computer Games Museum (Computerspielemuseum)
Focused on the history of games and how they have evolved over the years, including hands-on exhibits. Really fun!

Craft Beer Tour (advanced registration required)

German Technical Museum
Dedicated to transportation, manufacturing and technology, the museum boasts an impressive array of radios, phones, planes, sea-faring vessels and two whole locomotive sheds on its sprawling campus.

Berlin Trains

The closest train station to the Radial System venue is Berlin Ostbahnhof.
Metro: S-Bahn lines S3, S5, S7, S75 and S9
Warschauer Strasse lines: U1, U15 and U12
Alexanderplatz lines: U2, U5, U8

Rail

From Berlin Hauptbahnhof (main station) trains leave frequently to Berlin Ostbahnhof. It’s a 7-minute ride and from there a short 5-minute walk to the Radial System venue.

Bus

Berlin Ostbahnhof is served by bus lines 140, 147, 347.

Berlin Tegel Airport

There is no direct subway or train connection to Tegel Airport. Various buses and express buses operated by BVG (Berlin Transport Services) have stops located right outside Terminal A and B. Take TXL Bus / TXL JetExpressBus to Hauptbahnhof. From Hauptbahnhof S+U station take the S-Bahn S75 (direction S Wartenberg ) to Berlin Ostbahnhof. It is a short 5-minute walk to the Radial System venue.

Berlin Schönefeld Airport

Take the Airport Express (RE7 & RB14) regional train to Berlin Ostbahnhof. Travel Time: 15 minutes, trains run between 4:40am - 11:00pm. It is a short 5-minute walk to the Radial System venue.
